Personal Inflation Calculator

Print

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is the official measure of inflation in Australia. Each quarter, the CPI measures the change in the cost of a basket of goods and services. The CPI basket represents average expenditure for Australian households. However, your personal experience of inflation may be different due to differences in your spending habits. The Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) has developed a Personal Inflation Calculator which allows you to measure your own unique experience of inflation based on the goods and services you consume.

Disclaimer

The personal inflation rate based on the inflation calculator is provided as a guide to help you understand your unique experience of inflation, and should not be used as a substitute for the official CPI inflation rate.

Explanation

The official CPI inflation rate measures the average change in prices over the past year. The personal inflation rate is also calculated in this way but adjusted to reflect your individual spending habits. The calculator also allows you to enter monthly and annual expenses as you may only spend on an expenditure category once a year (for example, property rates). You can also enter expenses for individual capital cities or for the weighted average of the capital cities.

Each expenditure category represents a share of your overall spending. These expenditure shares are used as ‘weights’ in your basket of goods and services when calculating your personal inflation rate. If a category is blank, it won’t be included in your basket of goods and services. For example, the average amount spent by Australian households is 5.8% of their overall expenses on Rents. If you don’t rent, you won’t have any expenditure on rents and your basket of goods and services will exclude rents. If you spent more or less than 5.8% of your overall expenses on Rents, the calculator will take this into account and produce a different personal inflation rate.

The weights derived from your expenditure shares are then combined with the annual price movements from the official CPI to calculate your personal inflation rate.

Additional notes

The personal inflation calculator has been simplified for practical purposes.

  • The Housing group in the official CPI includes the purchase of new dwellings by owner occupiers (excluding land). However, for simplicity, the Housing group in the personal inflation calculator has been modified to include mortgage interest charges instead of new dwelling purchases.
  • The time series chart applies your current personal expenditure patterns to the last 5 years. However, the CPI is calculated using weights from a historical reference period. The CPI weights are updated periodically, currently every 12 months.
  • Some expenditure categories have been combined to simplify the entry of your household expenses.
  • Only published data available to the public are used in the calculations.
